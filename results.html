A Novel Radar Detection Prototype based on Constant False Alarm Rate Concepts

Results

The performance of the prototype Radar algorithm developed in this project will be evaluated using SNR vs PD curves. These graphs show the probability of detection for corresponding SNRs of different signals. Shown above is the traditional shape of an SNR vs PD curve. A signal with a lower SNR has an amplitude that is closer to the noise amplitude, which makes it harder to detect, resulting in a lower PD. On the other hand, a signal with a higher SNR has an amplitude further above the noise amplitude, making it easier to detect and increasing PD. As shown when comparing the two curves in the above image, the black curve clearly has a higher performance, as for each SNR, the algorithm represented by the black curve has a higher PD than the PD of the algorithm represented by the red curve. The same logic will be applied when comparing the performance of the prototype algorithm to other traditional algorithms like the CA-CFAR, GO-CFAR and SO-CFAR. Another graph shown below in the results is a clutter edge plot, which evaluated algorithm performance in a clutter edge environment. This environment contains a clutter wall, which is essentially 2 or more consecutive interferers of the same amplitude in the collected data. It is essentially a wall of interference as opposed to a singe interfering signal. These clutter edge plots evaluate an algorithm's false alarm rate for different clutter wall sizes. An area-based method of evaluation will also be used to test performance.

Conclusions

The CM-FOD-CFAR was tested for target detection performance in comparison to the FOD-CFAR and OSVI-CFAR as well as traditional CFAR algorithms in a variety of noise and clutter environments. A new method of target detection performance evaluation using area under curve calculations was also used, which quantified algorithm performance from 0 to 1. From the test results, it could be concluded that while the other algorithms varied in performance across the 4 environments tested, the CM-FOD-CFARâ€™s detection performance stayed consistent. Thus, the prototype maintained detection performance in all environments tested, indicating that the CM-FOD-CFAR was the most robust algorithm overall in this study. The high performance and robustness of the developed radar detection prototype algorithm (CM-FOD-CFAR) with its ability to adapt and perform across multiple environments in this study emphasize the importance of continuing to iterate on new ideas that could supersede present day radar detection techniques.
