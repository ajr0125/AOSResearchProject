A Novel Radar Detection Prototype based on Constant False Alarm Rate Concepts
  • Instagram
  • Medium

Abstract

The Constant False Alarm Rate (CFAR) family of algorithms are commonly used in radar detection systems to detect targets in the presence of background noise and clutter while maintaining a fixed probability of false alarm (how often a system falsely detects a target). However, would radar detection CFAR algorithms based on ordered statistics show a potential for improved performance in homogeneous and non-homogeneous environments?

This research project focuses on developing a novel prototype in Matlab based on CFAR theoretical concepts using advanced ordered statistics methods discussed in research papers about the First Order Difference CFAR (FOD-CFAR) and the Ordered Statistics Variability Index CFAR (OSVI-CFAR) to detect targets while minimizing detection of noise and clutter. The independent variable will be the method of target detection based on the prototype developed, the dependent variable will be the probability of detection, or the probability that a system will correctly detect a target, of the method of detection and the constant will be the probability of false alarm.

The prototype performance will be evaluated using simulation of the background environment with Gaussian and Weibull distributions, targets using the Swerling II model and Monte Carlo trials will be utilized to plot Pd (Probability of detection) vs SNR (signal-to-noise ratio) for a given PFA (Probability of False Alarm) in an attempt to provide valuable insights into building the next generation of robust radar detection algorithms.
